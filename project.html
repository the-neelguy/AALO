About Aalo

HELPING SOMEONE'S DREAM COME TRUE

Be the change

Aalo Organisation, an NGO for poor and needy section of the society, is an NGO in India directly benefitting over 15,00,00 homeless children and their families every year, through more than 200 live welfare projects on education, healthcare, livelihood and women empowerment, in over 600 remote villages, urban places and slums across 6 states of India.

We aim at educating young people, helping with monetary support, empowering women and organizing entertainment evenings in these bad times of pandemic.We at AALO try our best to save the poor sections of our society and raise them with honor and pride, educating each and every soul.

Raising funds

One of our main objective is to raise funds to directly help the people in need.

Arranging cultural evenings

We also look for the entertainment and well being of each and every people.

Mass Feeding

Aalo organizes mass feeding occasions frequently to increase our reach through the people.

EDUCATING PEOPLE

The Aalo Organisation Education society is a collective of organizers and popular educators from popular movements in India. Together we have decades of experience working in diverse communities of struggle, including organizing workers, immigrants, homeless people, survivors of police abuse, youth, faith communities, antiwar, and local peace economy activists.

  • Date: May 2020

HEALTH

We work in partnership with healthcare providers and local community health groups, delivering Advocacy Training and management which includes the integration of HHP’s Real World Data and Patient Education.

  • Date: May 2020

WOMEN EMPOWERMENT

AALO's Women Empowered Program (AWEP) supports the social and economic empowerment of women through community-based savings groups that help members save money, access credit, develop financial literacy and invest in income-generating activities. These women’s empowerment programs also promote skills building, self-esteem, access to information and resources, and community action that position women as confident decision-makers and leaders in their homes and communities.
